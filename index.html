STORY OF SHIRU INU

Once upon a time there lived a young couple, Shiba and Shina. They had a vision of bringing cryptocurrency to the world. So they decided to have a baby, whom they named Shila. Raising her was hard work but rewarding, for Shila immediately took to technology and all things crypto-related.

That's a story for another day! After Shila became old enough to look after herself, the couple had another baby whom they named Shiru.


At first, this boy had no clue what he was looking at, and certainly didn't understand any of the concepts. He only knew that it made his mom happy when he played with his smartphone or tablet. Then one day, his mother showed him a video of Bitcoin mining on YouTube. It didn't take long before he started asking questions about how cryptocurrencies worked and why people were so excited about them.

The other children in kindergarten were constantly trying to get his attention by talking to him about Pokemon Go or whatever else was popular at the moment. But because he seemed so interested in tech, they gravitated toward him naturally, and Shiba never had to worry about bullies picking on his son.



Shiru's parents were always very supportive of their son's interest in math and science, but even they couldn't help feeling envious when other kids would ask him to show off what he'd learned. Though they made sure he wasn't just playing games all day, they still spent most of their evenings teaching him new skills like basic coding and computer programming.


When Shiru finally became old enough to start elementary school, his parents enrolled him in a private STEM academyan institution designed specifically for cultivating children who could become future leaders in the digital age. There, he quickly excelled, scoring high marks every semester, and his family felt proud of him. He even won an award for "Most Likely to be the Next Satoshi!."


His sister had already mastered in crypto & had actively guided Shiru into it.

Shiru's parents always knew that their little boy was destined for great things, and as he grew older, he continued to prove them right.
